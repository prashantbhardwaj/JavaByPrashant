Keep it Simple: Avoid overwhelming beginners with complex concepts.
Hands-on Practice: Emphasize coding exercises and examples.
Visual Aids: Use diagrams and visual representations to explain concepts.
Q&A: Allocate time for questions and answers.
Break Down Concepts: Divide complex topics into smaller, manageable chunks.
Use simple examples: Avoid overly complex examples.
Reinforcement: At the beginning of each lesson, quickly recap the previous lesson.
Encourage questions: Make the students feel comfortable asking questions.
Utilize an online IDE: For the very first few lessons, an online IDE could be used to reduce the amount of initial setup the students have to do.
